Critical Questions About Enzyme Dental Care

What makes mutanase unique among dental enzymes?
October 2024 Copenhagen screening of 44 enzymes found mutanase as the only enzyme capable of removing biofilm independently. It specifically targets Î±-1,3-glucosidic bonds that form the primary structural component of water-insoluble glucan. Other enzymes like dextranase only work on secondary structures. The researchers tested combinations up to six enzymes - all effective formulations contained mutanase as the base.
How does the lactoperoxidase system work?
Lactoperoxidase catalyzes the oxidation of thiocyanate ions (SCN-) using hydrogen peroxide, producing hypothiocyanite (OSCN-). This compound has selective antimicrobial activity - it penetrates bacterial cell walls but doesn't harm human cells. The 2021 BMC study showed lozenges maintaining elevated OSCN- levels for 24 hours. Unlike antibiotics that create resistance, this system has been active in mammals for millions of years without bacterial adaptation.

Can enzymes replace professional cleanings?
No, but they dramatically improve outcomes. Enzymes dissolve soft biofilm matrix but can't remove calcified tartar.
