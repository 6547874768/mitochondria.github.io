How LED Teeth Whitening Really Works

LED teeth whitening combines hydrogen peroxide gel (typically 35%) with blue LED light at 480-520nm wavelength. The light supposedly accelerates peroxide breakdown into free radicals that break down tooth stains. Systems like MOON promise up to 12 shades whiter in 2 weeks.

However, research tells a different story. Multiple studies indicate the LED light adds minimal benefit beyond the peroxide alone. The real whitening comes from the high-concentration chemical, not the light. You're essentially paying $300-800 for a blue light that research suggests is largely unnecessary.

Evidence-Based Answers About LED Whitening

Does the LED light actually help?
Research shows mixed results. Studies indicate LED light provides minimal additional benefit beyond the peroxide gel alone. You're primarily paying for the chemical whitening agent, not the light effect.
Why do so many people get sensitivity?
The 35% hydrogen peroxide concentration penetrates enamel and reaches nerve endings. Studies show 54% experience mild sensitivity, 10% moderate, and 4% severe enough to discontinue treatment.
How long do results really last?
Research indicates results fade within 1-3 months if you consume staining foods and drinks. Without continuous treatments, teeth return to baseline color relatively quickly.
