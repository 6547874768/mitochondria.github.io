Quality Matters: Choosing Research-Grade Supplements

The mitochondrial supplement market is flooded with low-quality products that don't deliver the benefits seen in clinical studies. Understanding how to identify research-grade supplements ensures you get the same quality used in successful trials.

üîç Quality Assessment Criteria:

Bioavailability Forms

  • Ubiquinol vs. ubiquinone CoQ10 (ubiquinol better for those over 40)
  • R-lipoic acid vs. standard alpha-lipoic acid (R-form twice as bioavailable)
  • Acetyl-L-carnitine vs. L-carnitine (acetyl form crosses blood-brain barrier)
  • Micro-encapsulated or liposomal delivery systems for enhanced absorption

Third-Party Testing

  • Certificate of analysis (COA) verifying purity and potency
  • Heavy metal and contaminant testing
  • Dissolution and bioavailability testing
  • GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) certification

Research Backing

  • Companies that fund or participate in clinical research
  • Patented forms with published studies (like MitoQ, Kaneka Q10)
  • Transparent labeling with exact ingredient amounts
  • Evidence-based dosages matching successful clinical trials
