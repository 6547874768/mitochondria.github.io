While size enhancement is a myth, improving erectile function is absolutely possible through nitric oxide optimization. More nitric oxide means better blood flow, resulting in harder, more sustainable erections. This is why addressing hormonal health alongside vascular function provides optimal results.

Can any supplement actually increase penis size?
No. Zero. None. The American Urological Association is clear: "No pill, cream, or supplement can increase penis size." The penis is not a muscle that can be "grown" through nutrition. It's composed of specialized erectile tissue that reaches its final size after puberty. What CAN change is erection quality - better blood flow can make the penis appear larger at full erection, typically adding 0.5-1 inch in perceived size through improved rigidity.
What's the difference between nitric oxide supplements and ED drugs?
ED medications (Viagra, Cialis) are PDE5 inhibitors that prevent the breakdown of cGMP, maintaining erections. Nitric oxide supplements increase production of the gas that starts the entire process. Think of it this way: NO supplements turn on the faucet, ED drugs prevent the drain from opening. They work through different mechanisms and can be safely combined under medical supervision for synergistic effects - studies show 85% improvement with combination therapy.

Timeline varies by ingredient and individual. Beetroot extract and L-arginine can improve blood flow within 2-3 hours for exercise performance. Maximum benefits usually occur at 8-12 weeks. Morning erections often improve first - a good sign that nighttime vascular repair is enhanced.
Are there risks to male enhancement supplements?
Yes, particularly with unregulated products containing hidden ingredients. The FDA has found prescription ED drugs in over 300 "natural" male supplements. Side effects can include dangerous blood pressure drops, heart palpitations, and priapism. Stick to single-ingredient, third-party tested supplements. L-citrulline and L-arginine are generally safe but can lower blood pressure. Never combine with nitrate heart medications. Always inform your doctor about supplements, especially before surgery.
