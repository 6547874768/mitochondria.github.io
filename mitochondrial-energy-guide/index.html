Research indicates that when mitochondrial health declines, cellular energy production can slow dramatically, creating a cascade of health challenges.

Research indicates that mitochondrial function naturally declines with age due to accumulated oxidative damage, decreased efficiency of the electron transport chain, and reduced mitochondrial biogenesis. Studies suggest this age-related decline may begin as early as the third decade of life.
