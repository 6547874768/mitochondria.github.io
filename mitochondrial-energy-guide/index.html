Understanding Mitochondria: Your Body's Energy Factories

Mitochondria are specialized organelles that research shows are essential for cellular energy production. Studies indicate that mitochondria convert nutrients and oxygen into ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the primary energy currency used by all cells in your body.

Research describes mitochondria as "cellular powerhouses" because they generate approximately 90% of the ATP your body needs. Studies suggest that when mitochondrial function is optimal, cells operate efficiently. When research shows mitochondrial function declines—as studies indicate happens with aging—cellular energy production may be affected.
