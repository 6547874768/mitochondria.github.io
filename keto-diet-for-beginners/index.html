What Is the Keto Diet? Understanding the Basics

The ketogenic diet is a nutritional approach that research suggests may help the body enter a metabolic state called ketosis. Studies indicate that when carbohydrate intake is significantly reduced, the body may begin to burn fat for fuel instead of glucose.

Research shows that the ketogenic diet typically involves consuming very low carbohydrates, moderate protein, and high healthy fats. Studies suggest this macronutrient distribution may help shift the body's primary fuel source from carbohydrates to ketones, which are produced from fat.

Common Beginner Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Not Tracking Macros Carefully

Neglecting Electrolyte Balance
Solution: Increase sodium, potassium, and magnesium intake to prevent "keto flu"

Solution: Keep protein moderate (20-25% of calories) to maintain ketosis

Solution: Increase water intake as carb reduction affects hydration needs

Solution: Allow 2-4 weeks for adaptation before evaluating results
