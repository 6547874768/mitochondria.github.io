What is a Keto Diet? Understanding the Basics

The ketogenic diet is a high-fat, moderate-protein, and very low-carbohydrate eating plan that research indicates may help your body enter a metabolic state called ketosis. Studies suggest that when you significantly reduce carbohydrate intake, your body begins to burn fat for energy instead of glucose.

Research shows that the ketogenic diet typically consists of approximately 70-75% fat, 20-25% protein, and only 5-10% carbohydrates. This dramatic shift in macronutrient ratios is what studies indicate may trigger ketosis, a natural metabolic process where the liver produces ketones from fat.

📊 Standard Keto Diet Macronutrient Breakdown:

Fat:
70-75%
Protein:
20-25%
Carbs:
5-10%

Getting Started: Your Step-by-Step Keto Plan

Research indicates that successful keto implementation requires careful planning and gradual adaptation. Studies suggest that those who follow a systematic approach are more likely to achieve and maintain ketosis.

🚀 Your Keto Getting Started Checklist:

Step 1: Calculate Your Macros
Determine your daily calorie needs and calculate your fat, protein, and carb targets based on keto ratios
Step 2: Clean Out Your Pantry
Remove high-carb foods and stock up on keto-friendly options like healthy fats, low-carb vegetables, and quality proteins
Step 3: Plan Your Meals
Create a weekly meal plan that fits your macros and includes foods you enjoy eating
Step 4: Start Tracking
Use a food tracking app to monitor your carb intake and ensure you're staying within your limits
Step 5: Stay Hydrated
Increase your water intake and consider supplementing with electrolytes to prevent keto flu

Having a structured keto plan is essential for beginners. Studies show that people who follow organized approaches are more likely to stick with the diet long-term.

What to Eat on Keto: Foods to Enjoy and Avoid

Research indicates that food choices on keto are crucial for maintaining ketosis. Studies suggest that focusing on whole, nutrient-dense foods while avoiding high-carb options is key to success.

🍽️ Keto Food Categories:

Foods to Enjoy (Low-Carb, High-Fat)

  • Healthy fats: Avocados, olive oil, coconut oil, nuts, seeds
  • Quality proteins: Grass-fed beef, wild-caught fish, free-range eggs, poultry
  • Low-carb vegetables: Spinach, kale, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini
  • Full-fat dairy: Butter, cheese, heavy cream, Greek yogurt
  • Berries: Blackberries, raspberries, strawberries (in moderation)

Foods to Avoid (High-Carb)

  • Grains: Wheat, rice, oats, quinoa, bread, pasta
  • Sugary foods: Candy, soda, fruit juice, desserts
  • Starchy vegetables: Potatoes, corn, peas, carrots
  • Most fruits: Bananas, apples, oranges, grapes
  • Legumes: Beans, lentils, chickpeas
