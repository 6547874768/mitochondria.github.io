Why NAD+ Crashes After 40

The precipitous drop in NAD+ after 40 isn't random - it's driven by specific biological mechanisms that converge at this critical age. Understanding these drivers helps explain why so many people experience a sudden health decline in their fifth decade.

CD38 explosion: The enzyme CD38, which destroys NAD+, increases dramatically with age. Research shows CD38 levels can rise 2-3 fold between ages 30 and 60. Since one molecule of CD38 can degrade hundreds of NAD+ molecules, this creates a devastating drain on cellular reserves. It's like having a leak in your fuel tank that gets progressively worse.

PARP hyperactivation: DNA damage accumulates with age, triggering PARP enzymes that consume massive amounts of NAD+ for repair processes. After 40, this consumption often outpaces production, creating a deficit. Mitochondrial dysfunction amplifies this problem, as damaged mitochondria leak reactive oxygen species that cause more DNA damage.

Salvage pathway decline: Your body's ability to recycle and produce NAD+ weakens significantly after 40. The enzyme NAMPT, crucial for NAD+ synthesis, decreases by up to 50% in aging tissues. Combined with increased consumption, this creates a perfect storm of NAD+ depletion that accelerates all aspects of aging.
